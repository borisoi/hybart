Performance at the Brian Lake Bowl Theater, April 7 2019. Katya Oicherman, Nooshin Hakim, Pedram Baldari, Boris Oicherman, Rachel Jendrzejewski. Original concept and text by Katya Oicherman. Curated by Boris Oicherman. Produced by David Harris. With support from [Rimon: The Minnesota Jewish Arts Counsil](https://jewishminneapolis.org/do-something-jewish/join-a-group/rimon-2/).

Truth is produced in conversations. That is, truth is in flux: it depends on who is speaking to whom. Kitchen conversations, bar intimations, classroom debates, panel discussions, artist talks and interviews - those are just modes of the production of truth. The significance of any particular subject is not a given: we talk to each other to find out what is important to us.

In Judaism, a conversation is a form of study and legislation. The Jewish law is historically accompanied by “sheelot ve tshuvot”, questions and answers, or *responsa*, wherein rabbis reply to questions, on subjects ranging from mundane to theological.

Respons-a! responded to that tradition. It is a game of special cards set to poetically and humorously address both mundane and “big” questions in everyday life and art.  Each of the 80 cards offered a set of different questions that one player asked the other. While the questions were predetermined, the answers depended entirely on the responding player.
